Modern monochrome cinematic image style, heavily atmospheric and minimal, with a soft analog-film look. The image should feel like a quiet real-life moment captured late at night or early morning, distant, reflective, nostalgic, beautiful, very dreamy, misty and atmospheric. Use only black, white, and deep gray tones, with no color accents. The lighting should be diffuse, hazy, and low-contrast overall, with one soft practical light source bleeding through fog, mist, rain, glass, or street haze.

The visual treatment should be blurry, photographic, and impressionistic, as if shot on an old camera through wet glass, condensation, fogged optics, or a slightly degraded film lens. Avoid overly sharp edges, fantasy-like shapes, or mythic symbolism. Forms should feel real but softened by atmosphere, distance, motion blur, grain, and imperfect exposure. Use strong film grain, visible dithering, halftone-like noise, subtle compression artifacts, and analog texture across the whole image. The grain should feel intentional and aesthetic, like a degraded monochrome still from experimental cinema or a low-resolution documentary photograph.

The contrast should be restrained but cinematic: deep black shadows, smoky midtones, and glowing white highlights that bloom softly into the surrounding haze. Use soft vignetting around the edges and a wide, empty, atmospheric composition. The image should feel modern and urban, but not clean or digital — more like a quiet real-world scene seen through memory, rain, and old film.

Surfaces should feel wet, reflective, foggy, and grounded in reality: asphalt, concrete, glass, metal, water, pavement, windows, or a shallow reflective street surface. Reflections should be soft, elongated, blurred, and fading naturally into the grain. Clouds, fog, smoke, rain, and background shapes should be layered and photographic, with large hazy masses becoming smaller and more distant as they recede toward the horizon. The entire style should be poetic, minimal, contemporary, cinematic, and emotionally quiet, but still believable as a real photographed scene.
